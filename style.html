What are Solar Panel Subsidies?

Solar panel subsidies are financial incentives or support mechanisms provided by the government to encourage the installation and use of solar panels for generating electricity. These subsidies aim to make solar energy more affordable for homeowners, thereby accelerating the adoption of renewable energy sources.

Why Go Solar?

Going solar is a powerful choice for individuals and businesses looking to save on energy costs, contribute to environmental sustainability, and increase energy independence. Switching to solar energy is now more affordable than ever, thanks to government-backed subsidies and incentives.

Key Benefits of Solar Subsidies

  • Lower Costs: Reduce the initial investment in solar panels through government subsidies.
  • Eco-Friendly: Solar energy is a clean, renewable resource that helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions and reliance on fossil fuels. It lessens pollution and conserves water, a key factor since traditional electricity production often requires vast amounts of water.
  • Increased Savings: Solar panels can significantly reduce electricity bills. With energy prices on the rise, generating power from sunlight can save money over the long term, even making homes and businesses eligible for tax credits, subsidies, or rebates in many regions.
  • Energy Independence: With solar, individuals and communities can become more energy-resilient. Solar power ensures that users can generate their own electricity, reducing dependence on the grid and providing backup during power outages, especially with battery storage solutions.
  • Job Creation and Economic Growth:The solar industry creates thousands of jobs annually in installation, manufacturing, and maintenance, supporting economic growth and fostering innovation in renewable energy sectors.

Types of Solar Subsidies Available

Government Grants

Grants are direct financial aids provided by the government to cover part of the installation costs of solar energy systems. These grants are often offered to homeowners, businesses, or community organizations, and are particularly aimed at making renewable energy adoption accessible. Grants do not need to be repaid, making them one of the most attractive financial aids for solar energy, though they are often limited and competitive.

Tax Incentives

Tax incentives, such as credits and deductions, reduce the amount of tax owed based on the cost or energy production of a solar installation. For example, the U.S. offers the federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC), which provides a percentage-based deduction on installation costs. This incentive can apply to both residential and commercial systems, encouraging wider adoption by reducing the net cost of solar projects.

Low-Interest Loans

Many governments and financial institutions offer low-interest loans specifically for renewable energy projects, which provide financing at rates significantly lower than standard loans. These loans allow property owners to spread the costs over time, making solar installations financially feasible without a high upfront investment. Government-backed programs often support these loans, providing better terms and manageable repayment options.

How to get solar panel subsidy in India?

The Indian government, through the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE), often offers various schemes for promoting solar energy. Gather all necessary documents required for solar subsidy application. This typically includes identification proof, address proof, and proof of property ownership.

Step 1: Download the Sandes App from the National subsidy portal

Step 2: Register on the national subsidy portal and submit the application.

Ensure that the applicant’s name is the same as in the recent electricity bill. Uploading a recent electricity bill is mandatory.

Step 3: You will receive feasibility approval from DISCOM via registered email ID.

Step 4: Once the solar panel installation is completed, a work completion report and the net meter installation request need to be submitted

Step 5: After installation of the net meter and inspection by DISCOM, DISCOM will generate a commissioning certificate from the portal.

Step 6: Upon receipt of the commissioning report, submit bank account details and a cancelled cheque through the portal.

Step 7: Subsidy is disbursed to the bank account submitted by you.

What are the eligibility criteria for the National rooftop solar scheme in 2024?

To be eligible for the National Rooftop Solar Scheme in 2024, applicants must meet the following criteria:

• Be a permanent citizen of India

• Be over 18 years old

• Have their own house

• Have the necessary documents

• Have an annual income of less than Rs 1 or 1.5 lakh

• Not be involved in government service

• The maximum capacity for availing subsidy on solar panel is 10kW.

To install solar panels for homes and businesses, consumers can pay a down payment of 20%–30%. They will need to provide income proof, such as:

•ITR files for the last three years

•The most recent electricity bill for the last six months

•KYC documents required for solar panel installation

How much subsidy of solar panels do we get in India?

In a significant move to boost solar energy adoption among residential consumers, the Indian government has announced a 23% increase in the Central Financial Assistance (CFA) subsidy as of January 2024. This enhanced solar panel subsidy is designed to make solar installations more accessible and affordable for homeowners across the country.

When does the solar panel subsidy end?

The government has extended its initiative of solar subsidy until March 31, 2026. Under this government solar panel scheme 2024, homeowners who install solar panels on their rooftops before this deadline are eligible for financial incentives. This initiative is a critical component of a broader strategy aimed at increasing the adoption of solar energy. The continuation of this support is contingent upon the fulfillment of the scheme’s primary objectives.
